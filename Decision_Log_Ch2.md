Decision 002: Cairo Infrastructure Strategy

Date: 25 February 2026
Decision Maker: Team_01 

Context: Cairo data center cannot support AI engine computational load

Strategic Assessment

Moving to a cloud provider may violate Egypt’s data sovereignty regulations if medical data is stored outside national borders. Additionally, dependency on external vendors creates operational risk in case of outages during medical emergencies.

Economic Assessment

Over 4 years, both options cost $2M.
Over 10 years, Build costs $2M while Buy costs $5M, making Build more cost-effective long-term.

Technical Assessment

The Build option requires sufficient infrastructure management skills within the Cairo IT team. While major providers such as Amazon Web Services and Microsoft Azure offer compliant and scalable solutions, data location remains a critical technical constraint.

Risk Assessment

Regulatory changes may force data migration if cloud laws tighten. Build introduces procurement delay risk, while Buy introduces vendor dependency and scalability cost risks.

Decision: Option 1 – Build (Upgrade Cairo Servers)
Justification

We select Option 1 (Build) to ensure compliance with Egyptian data sovereignty requirements and maintain full control over sensitive medical data. From a long-term economic perspective, Build provides a lower Total Cost of Ownership over 10 years compared to the cloud subscription model. Although the 6-month delay introduces schedule risk, it is manageable within the overall project timeline.

Risk Mitigation

Initiate early hardware procurement planning.

Provide technical training for Cairo IT staff.

Apply phased deployment to minimize operational disruption.
