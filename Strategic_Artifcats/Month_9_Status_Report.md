## Section 3: Root Cause Analysis — Berlin Site Failure

The Berlin site is the primary driver of project underperformance due 
to a critical **Requirement Gap** identified in Task BER-001. The original 
estimate assumed that AI training data received from Cairo hospitals would 
be clean and require minimal preprocessing; however, this assumption proved 
invalid, resulting in significant unplanned rework on the AI Analytics Engine. 
This gap caused a cascade of delays across the Spiral Model development 
cycles in Berlin, consuming additional time and budget that were not 
accounted for in the original Estimation Basis approved on 15 March 2026.

---

## Section 4: The Pivot — Administrative Actions

**Action 1: Fast-Tracking the Bangalore Nurse UI**
The Bangalore Mobile Nurse Interface (BAN-001) will be fast-tracked by 
running design and development sprints in parallel to recover schedule 
slippage. Since BAN-001 assumed no biometric authentication is required, 
the scope remains stable and fast-tracking is low-risk, recovering an 
estimated 3 weeks on the critical path.

**Action 2: Descoping the AI Chatbot Feature**
The AI-powered chatbot feature will be formally descoped from the current 
release and deferred to Phase 2. This recovers budget and redirects 
senior AI developer resources from Berlin to address the Cairo data 
preprocessing issue directly, targeting the root cause of the Requirement Gap.
