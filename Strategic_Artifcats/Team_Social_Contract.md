## Section 1: Conflict Resolution Protocol

When a "Site War" or inter-team conflict arises, we follow this 3-step process:

**Step 1 — Name the Issue, Not the Person:**
State what the technical or process disagreement is,
without attributing blame to an individual or site.
Example: "There is a conflict about the API response format"
NOT: "The Berlin team wrote a bad API"

**Step 2 — Neutral Ground Discussion:**
Schedule a dedicated 30-minute call (separate from the sprint)
to address the conflict. Both parties present their perspective.
The PM or Coordinator facilitates — they do not judge.

**Step 3 — SOP Creation:**
Every resolved conflict produces a documented Standard Operating
Procedure. This becomes the binding norm for all future similar
situations. It is logged in Decisions_Log.md.

**Escalation Path:** If the conflict is not resolved within 3 days,
it escalates to the London PMO for final arbitration.

## Section 2: Communication Norms

**Golden Window Hours:**
All 4 sites must be available simultaneously during:
Monday–Thursday | 10:00–12:00 GMT
(Cairo: 12:00–14:00 EET | Berlin: 11:00–13:00 CET |
Bangalore: 15:30–17:30 IST | London: 10:00–12:00 GMT)

**24-Hour Response Rule:**
Any Slack message or Jira comment from another site
must receive at least an acknowledgment within 24 hours.
"Seen — will respond by [date]" is an acceptable acknowledgment.

**Meeting Protocol:**
- All meetings have an agenda shared 24 hours in advance
- Maximum meeting size: 6 people (to control communication channels)
- Decisions are always documented in the meeting notes within 2 hours

**Escalation for Blocked Items:**
If any developer is blocked for more than 4 hours, they
must post a blocker flag in Jira and tag the PM directly.
Silent blocking is a Social Contract violation.

## Section 3: Ethical Commitment

**Handling "Ignore a Bug" Requests:**
If any team member receives a request — from a manager,
stakeholder, or anyone — to ignore, hide, or deprioritize
a known bug or security issue, the following applies:

Per the ACM/IEEE PUBLIC Principle:
"Software engineers shall act consistently with the public
interest. Bugs affecting patient health or data security
are NEVER acceptable to suppress."

Our Commitment:
1. Any known bug, especially one affecting patient data
or system security, MUST be reported — regardless of
who asks you to stay quiet.
2. We maintain a private Jira issue type "CONFIDENTIAL-BUG"
for sensitive disclosures that should not appear in
the public backlog.
3. No team member will face retaliation for reporting a
legitimate technical concern. This is a "Safe Harbor."
4. The PM is ethically obligated to report all known issues
to the GlobalCare Board — even when the news is bad.
